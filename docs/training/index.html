Orientation

How it all fits together

Three layers. You set up your business once; you run many campaigns on top of it; and the whole machine can improve itself over time.

The system
The engine + the System Manager. Improves itself over time (you capture ideas and run retros).
Your business — set up once (Phase 0)
Your brand voice, look, the rules you follow, and where you publish. Every campaign inherits this.
Each campaign — Phases 1–6
Brief → concepts → plan → produce → launch → keep going. As many as you like, each in its own chat.
Lessons graduate up — what a campaign learns improves your foundation.Context inherits down — every campaign starts from your foundation.

The first thing you do — "Onboard my business" — fills this in. It isn't an app screen; it's a folder of files in your repo that every campaign reads from. The interview gathers the information below, and campaigns can't start until it's in place.

Phases 5–6 · Launch & cadence

The weekly cadence — what to do each week

Once you're live, the campaign runs on a simple rhythm — each week has one or two moves. The dashboard's Phase 6 row is the real tracker (it counts the steps done in campaign.yaml); the checkboxes here are your personal one. The steps stay scannable; the detailed how-to sits in collapsible blocks.

campaigns/acme-q3-leadgen/operations.html
WeekWhat you do 1
Wk 1Publish the launch post → LinkedIn. Then keep half an eye on it daily (~5 min) — demos booking, page live, nothing broken. No decisions this week.
Wk 3 · Day 5The first read (~30–45 min). Walk every number; only small, reversible tweaks — no big budget moves (that's Wk 4).
Wk 3Publish the case-study carousel → LinkedIn.
Wk 4The big call — reallocation (~60–90 min). Shift budget from the weakest channel to the strongest, each kept above its floor. Write down why, not just what.
Wk 5Publish the demo-recap email, and keep reading (apply any Wk-4 changes).
Wk 6Close the campaign (~2–3 hr): pull the numbers → results report → run the retro + post an honest LinkedIn recap → graduate the learnings → freeze.
▸ Publishing a post — for whoever posts to LinkedIn 2
Each post ships with a ready caption and image, so whoever publishes just copies and posts. Caption: 01-launch/caption-seed.md · image: …/01-launch/hero.png. After posting, mark it done — open campaign.yaml and set the matching cadence: entry from status: pending to done (that's what the dashboard's Phase 6 row counts).
→ Every asset for this campaign lives in the Gallery; the cadence just tells you when to ship each one and when to read the numbers. (A "6-week cycle at a glance" timeline view is there too, for the one-screen picture.)
  1. 1
    One or two moves a week — publish, read, decide, or close. Tick them off as your personal tracker; the dashboard's Phase 6 row is the system's own count.
  2. 2
    The how-to, one click away — each step's detail (captions, image paths, where to mark it done) lives in a collapsible block, so the weekly list stays scannable.

Improving the system itself

The System Manager
