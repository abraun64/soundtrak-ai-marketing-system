Getting started

From a fresh install to your first finished piece of marketing. You mostly answer questions and approve work, in chat — the system does the heavy lifting. To see what each screen looks like, keep the Training guide open alongside.

Four things worth knowing first:
  1. You're in charge. The system drafts and checks the work, but nothing goes out without your yes. Push back whenever something feels off.
  2. It works in steps, on purpose. It won't let you skip ahead — you can't start a campaign before it knows your business. The foundation makes everything after it faster and better.
  3. Do the foundation once. A little effort up front pays off on everything that follows.
  4. One chat per job. Start a fresh chat for each campaign, and another for each system retro. State lives in files, so a new chat always picks up where you left off — and separate chats keep the AI focused.

Before you start

  • You've finished the deployment guide and the system said READY.
  • You've opened the system folder in Claude Code and can see the chat box.

Part 1 — Tell it about your business (Phase 0)

The foundation every campaign builds on. In chat, type "Onboard <your business name>". It interviews you and drafts each piece for your approval — you answer, then say "yes" or "change this":

  • Your brand — how you sound and look (voice, tone, style guide, logo).
  • Your positioning & segments — what you stand for, and who you're selling to.
  • Rules you must follow — only if you're regulated (finance, health…): disclaimers, things you can't claim.
  • Where you publish + your martech — channels, plus your tools (CRM, email, analytics). If any needs a password or key, the system shows you where to enter it safely — never in a file.
  • Best-practice examples — a few standout pieces for the system to learn from.

You'll know it's done when the system says you can start a campaign. (Try before then and it gently stops you — that's the step it's protecting.)

Part 2 — Your first campaign (Phases 1–3)

Type "start a campaign". It asks for your yes at three points:

  • The brief (Phase 1) — one goal, one measure of success, who it's for, the offer. You approve.
  • The ideas (Phase 2) — three creative directions, one recommended. You pick.
  • The plan (Phase 3) — exactly what it will make. You approve.

Part 3 — Making the pieces (Phase 4)

  • For each item in the plan, the system writes it, checks it against your brand (and your rules, if regulated), fixes issues, and shows you a clean preview.
  • You approve each finished piece — or send it back with a note.

Part 4 — Getting it out, and keeping it going (Phases 5–6)

  • Each approved piece either publishes (where it's connected) or gives you simple copy-paste steps. You do the final "send."
  • Anything ongoing (a weekly email) runs on a simple weekly cadence — the hard setup is already done.

Part 5 — Keeping it improving (the System Manager)

The system can get better at your business over time. In a fresh chat:

  • Capture ideas as they occur — "system idea: …". It files them; you don't have to act now.
  • Run a retro after a campaign or every few weeks — "run a system retro". It turns the lessons into a prioritised to-do list and tracks the history.
When something feels wrong — tell the system in plain words ("this doesn't sound like us", "why did it do that?"). Usually the fix is to adjust your brand profile (say "update my brand") or rephrase what you asked — not to fight it. Still stuck? See Help & guides.
