## Composition principles

### Rule of thirds

Divide the canvas into a 3×3 grid. Place focal points on the intersection points (the "power points"). Used as a default starting framework — break deliberately for effect.

### Golden ratio (1:1.618)

Spiral composition that places focal points along a logarithmic curve. Works well for natural-feeling, organic compositions. More subtle than rule of thirds.

### Focal point hierarchy

- **One primary focal point** — where the eye lands first
- **One or two secondary focal points** — where the eye travels next
- **Supporting elements** — fill, texture, balance

If two elements claim primary status, the composition feels unresolved. Demote one.

### Balance

- **Symmetrical balance**: mirrored composition; formal, classical, premium-coded
- **Asymmetrical balance**: weighted composition; dynamic, modern, energetic
- **Radial balance**: elements emanate from a center point; less common in marketing, more common in editorial

### Negative space (figure / ground)

The space *around* a subject is as important as the subject. Generous negative space reads as premium, calm, confident. Crowded compositions read as urgent, busy, value-coded.

### Sight-lines

Faces, body posture, arrows, and even abstract diagonals create implicit sight-lines. Use sight-lines to direct attention — a face looking at the headline pulls the viewer's eye to it.

### Composition for fast-glance

Social tiles and OOH have 1–3 seconds. Composition rules for fast-glance:

- One subject, one message
- High contrast
- Bold typography integrated into the composition (not separate from)
- No more than 3 distinct elements competing for attention

### Composition for dwell

Web heroes, presentation slides, posters have longer dwell. Composition rules for dwell:

- Layered hierarchy (the viewer can find more on second look)
- Considered negative space
- Detail that rewards close attention
- A primary focal point + supporting elements that complete the story

## Grid systems

### Column grids

- **12-column grid**: web standard; flexible (6+6, 4+8, 3+9, 4+4+4, etc.)
- **8-column grid**: editorial / magazine
- **6-column grid**: simpler layouts; product / app design
- **3-column grid**: print, mobile

### Modular grids

- Cells arranged in rows AND columns
- Powerful for data-heavy layouts (dashboards, infographics, complex editorial)

### Baseline grid

- Vertical rhythm — every line of text sits on a consistent baseline
- 4 or 8 px baseline grid is common
- Tight discipline; high visual coherence when applied

### Grid discipline

- **Establish the grid before composing**
- **Snap elements to the grid** by default
- **Break the grid deliberately** for emphasis — a "bleed" element that crosses a column line creates intentional tension

## Typography as visual element

Beyond legibility, typography is a primary visual carrier in static design.

### Type as image

- Large-scale headline type often the dominant visual element
- Type can BE the design (typography-led posters, editorial covers, branded social tiles)

### Type pairings (recap from visual-craft-shared)

- One display + one body is the default
- Pair by contrast (serif + sans; geometric + humanist)
- Three fonts max

### Type-led composition rules

- **Tight tracking** on display headlines = bold, modern, premium
- **Loose tracking** on small text = caption / label / editorial
- **All caps with letterspacing** = display use only (never body)
- **Mixed case** = body and most headlines
- **Italics for emphasis** sparingly

### Setting numbers

- **Tabular figures** (monospaced digits) for data tables, financial figures
- **Old-style figures** (varying x-height digits) for body text in literary contexts
- **Lining figures** (uniform-height) for headlines and most modern UI

## Color in static design

### Palette discipline

- **3–5 colors max** in a single composition
- **One primary**, **one or two secondary**, **one or two accents**
- **Accent color used sparingly** — 5–10% of the composition; signals where to look

### Color and hierarchy

- Saturated colors advance (come forward)
- Desaturated colors recede
- Use this physically — accent on the primary focal point; muted background

### Color and emotion

- Choose the palette to match the emotional register of the work
- Warm palettes (red / orange / yellow) feel energetic, urgent, friendly
- Cool palettes (blue / green / purple) feel calm, professional, trustworthy
- Monochromatic palettes feel refined
- Complementary palettes feel charged

### Color in motion vs static

- Static palettes can be more saturated; motion benefits from slightly lower saturation to avoid eye fatigue across frames

## White space discipline

White space (or "negative space") is not empty — it's an active design element.

### Functions of white space

- **Hierarchy creator**: isolating an element gives it weight
- **Breathing room**: prevents visual claustrophobia
- **Premium signal**: generous white space reads as confident and high-end
- **Reading aid**: white space between blocks of text guides scanning

### White space rules

- **Margins**: at least 5% of canvas edge (more for premium-coded work; less for value-coded)
- **Gutters**: consistent spacing between elements (use spacing tokens)
- **Around the focal point**: more white space draws more attention
- **Between sections**: visual breaks signal "new idea"

### Anti-pattern

- Filling every pixel because empty space "feels wasted" — that's exactly what white space is supposed to do

## Format-tagged sections

### Social feed tile

**Single-image post on Instagram / LinkedIn / Facebook / Threads.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Aspect ratio | 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait, IG max) |
| Resolution | 1080×1080 (square) or 1080×1350 (portrait) |
| Glance time | 1.5–3 sec |
| Primary platform expectations | LinkedIn: text-led acceptable; IG: visual-led; FB: photo-led |

**Composition rules**:

- **One subject, one message** — the post does ONE thing visually
- **Text-on-image** if used: high contrast, readable on thumbnail (when post is shown at 200×200 in feed scroll)
- **Brand-recognisable in 1 sec** — visual register signals the brand even before headline lands
- **Hook in visual**, not buried in caption — viewer decides to stop or scroll based on the tile

**Common patterns**:

- Quote graphic (typography-only with author attribution)
- Data graphic (single statistic visualised)
- Brand-photo with headline overlay
- Illustration / iconography-led conceptual visual
- Personality post (face-led portrait + framing copy)

### Social carousel design

**Multi-slide swipeable deck on Instagram / LinkedIn / TikTok.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Aspect ratio | 1:1 or 4:5 (IG); 1.91:1 horizontal or 1:1 (LinkedIn PDF) |
| Slide count | 7–10 slides; 9 is sweet spot |
| Glance time per slide | 1.5–3 sec for swipe decision |

**Cover slide composition**:

- Bigger / bolder type than interior slides
- More color contrast
- A clear visual hook — pattern, contradiction, intrigue
- 3–8 word headline
- Brand recognisable

**Interior slide composition**:

- 3–8 word headline + 1–3 short body lines
- Consistent visual register (don't change style every slide)
- Each slide earns the next swipe (incomplete information, building tension, sequencing logic)
- Use scale, color, or layout shifts to signal beat changes (e.g. setup → reframe → payoff)

**Final slide composition**:

- Unambiguous CTA — save / follow / book / read / etc.
- Visually distinct from interior slides (mirror cover slide's energy if returning to the brand register)
- Often includes a small "next steps" block

**Coherence rules**:

- All slides share the brand's type system + color palette
- Layout grid consistent across slides
- Visual register can vary (one slide can be photo-led, next typography-led) but composition rhythm must feel intentional, not random

### Display banner

**IAB-standard banner ads for programmatic display.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Common sizes | 728×90, 300×250, 320×50, 160×600, 970×250, 300×600 |
| Glance time | 0.5–1.5 sec |
| Sound | none |
| Motion | static or light HTML5 animation |

**Composition rules**:

- **One headline, one image, one CTA** — display ads work like mini-billboards
- **Brand prominent** — logo visible, often top-left or right
- **CTA button visually distinct** — color contrast pulls the click
- **No more than 3 distinct elements** competing
- **Animation if HTML5**: 3–5 second loop; final frame is the CTA; respect frequency caps

### Billboard / OOH

**Out-of-home: highway billboards, transit, wild posting, street furniture.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Aspect ratios | 14:48 (US bulletin), 6-sheet (UK portrait), 48-sheet (UK landscape), 9:16 / 16:9 digital |
| Glance time | 1.5–3 sec (highway); 5–15 sec (transit) |
| Word count ceiling | 6–8 words (highway); 12–20 (transit at-eye-level) |
| Print constraints | High-DPI requirements (often 100dpi at output size = massive files) |

**Composition rules**:

- **One idea, one image, one line of copy** — that's it
- **Bold typography** — readable at distance and motion
- **High contrast** between elements
- **Brand mark visible** at glance
- **No CTA URL unless memorable** — billboards don't drive direct response; QR codes rarely scanned in motion (but increasingly useful in transit / waiting contexts)

**Color discipline**:

- Saturation works on OOH where it would feel garish on a screen
- Test legibility at 50% scale (at distance / at motion, that's effectively what the viewer sees)

### Web hero

**Top-of-fold visual on landing / homepage / product page.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Aspect ratio | Responsive — designed mobile-first, desktop secondary; common ratios 16:9 desktop, 4:5 or 9:16 mobile crop |
| Glance time | 3–8 sec for hero comprehension |
| Dwell time | 30s–5+ min if engaged |

**Composition rules**:

- **Headline + subhead + CTA + visual** — these four elements drive most heroes
- **Visual supports the headline** — doesn't compete with it
- **Mobile-first composition** — the hero on mobile is 5× more important than desktop
- **CTA visually distinct** — button color, button placement
- **Trust signals near hero** — logo strip, testimonial pull-quote, rating
- **Above-fold-only** for the most critical elements — everything else is a scroll away

**Visual style choices**:

- Product hero (the product is the visual)
- Founder / team hero (humans + face)
- Concept hero (abstract illustration / pattern)
- Customer-in-use hero (real photography of customers)
- Data hero (a chart or statistic dramatised)

### Email visual

**Static or animated header / inline visuals in marketing emails.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Width | 600–640px max |
| Format | JPEG / PNG / GIF (no WebP — Outlook doesn't support) |
| File size | <100KB per image; <1MB total email |
| Image:text ratio | 60:40 text:image to avoid spam |

**Composition rules**:

- **Hero image at top** — 600×200 to 600×400 typical
- **Don't put critical info ONLY in images** — image-blocking is widespread (Outlook default, mobile data-saver)
- **Alt text on every image** — accessibility + image-blocked fallback
- **Text-on-image readable at mobile width** (~320px) — what looks good at 600px desktop is illegible on mobile

**Animated header (GIF)**:

- 3–5 second loop max
- Subtle motion (parallax, light text reveal, gentle camera move) outperforms aggressive motion
- File size discipline — GIFs balloon fast; optimise

### Presentation slide

**Slides for TEDx / keynote / webinar / town hall / scripted talks.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 standard; 4:3 legacy; 1:1 for some creative talks |
| Dwell time per slide | 30 sec – 3 min (talk-paced, not viewer-paced) |
| Glance time | n/a (captive audience) |

**Composition rules**:

- **One idea per slide** — slides support the speaker, not replace them
- **Minimal text** — bullet-point slides are the failure mode; large headline or single visual per slide is the goal
- **Big type** — readable from the back of the room (24pt minimum body, 48pt+ headlines)
- **High contrast** — lighting in venues varies; high contrast survives bad projection
- **No animations** unless they earn the moment (a chart building up; a comparison reveal)
- **Image-led slides** outperform text-led slides for retention

**Sub-form variations**:

- **Title slide**: brand + talk name + speaker
- **Section slides**: visual marker for transitions between sections
- **Content slides**: the bulk; one idea each
- **Build slides**: progressive reveal (one item at a time)
- **Comparison slides**: side-by-side; before / after; us / them
- **Quote slides**: a memorable line + attribution
- **End slide**: CTA + how to follow up / contact

### Poster

**Print poster — gallery, event, brand, music, film, political.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Aspect ratio | Varies: A1, A2, A3, US Tabloid, 24×36, 18×24 |
| Glance time | 5–30 sec depending on placement and audience |
| Print | High DPI (300+); CMYK; consider substrate (matte / gloss / textured) |

**Composition rules**:

- **Visual hierarchy bold** — posters are studied; they reward deep composition
- **Typography often the work** — many great posters are typography-led
- **Iconic image** — if image-led, one strong image vs many small images
- **Information hierarchy clear** — title / date / venue / detail
- **Edge-to-edge composition** — posters often bleed; full-bleed reads more confident than margined

### Infographic / data visualisation

**Static visual representation of data / process / system.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Aspect ratio | Varies — vertical (social-friendly) or landscape (web-friendly) |
| Length | Often long-form vertical; 1080×4320 (3× standard portrait) is common for social |

**Composition rules**:

- **Data integrity first** — never distort data to look better (truncated axes, misleading scales, cherry-picked time ranges)
- **One question per chart** — charts that try to answer 3 questions answer none
- **Labels readable** — axis labels, units, data labels
- **Color used semantically** — same data series, same color; semantic color (green = good, red = bad) used consciously
- **Annotations** — call out the insight, don't make the viewer derive it
- **Source cited** — data provenance visible

**Chart-type discipline**:

- **Bar chart**: compare quantities across categories
- **Line chart**: show change over time
- **Stacked bar**: parts of a whole over categories
- **Pie chart**: parts of a whole at a single point in time (avoid >5 segments; consider a donut)
- **Scatter**: relationship between two variables
- **Heatmap**: density / intensity across a 2D grid
- **Sankey**: flow between categories
- **Treemap**: hierarchical proportional area

### Print ad

**Magazine, newspaper full-page or fractional ads.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Format | Full-page bleed, double-page spread, half-page, quarter-page |
| Resolution | 300dpi at final print size |
| Color | CMYK; spot colors for brand-critical |

**Composition rules**:

- **Headline does the heavy lifting** — 8 words or fewer for full-page
- **Image plus headline** is the dominant pattern; sometimes typography-only
- **Body copy if needed** — 30–150 words; readable but not the main work
- **Logo placement** — usually bottom-right or top-right; never compete with headline
- **Whitespace generous** — print magazines reward calm composition

### Packaging design

**Product packaging — bottle, box, label, sleeve.**

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Shape | Product-determined |
| Glance time | 1–2 sec (shelf scan) |
| Print | Pantone matching, substrate decisions, structural design (die-cuts, folds) |

**Composition rules**:

- **Brand mark + product name + key claim** — the front-of-pack hierarchy
- **Hierarchy at shelf** — brand recognisable at 6 ft, claim readable at 3 ft, ingredients at 1 ft
- **Color signals category** — beverages, supplements, consumer electronics each have category conventions
- **Back-of-pack** holds ingredients, claims, instructions, legal — dense but organised
- **Differentiation at shelf** — your pack vs the competitive set; what's distinct?

## Variant generation across formats

When the same campaign needs 1:1 / 4:5 / 9:16 / 16:9 / billboard / web hero variants from one master composition.

### Don't: stretch / crop arbitrarily

- 16:9 hero cropped to 9:16 = subject cut off
- 1:1 tile stretched to 4:1 banner = distorted

### Do: redesign for each format

- **Master composition**: the canonical visual
- **Per-format variants**: redesigned to fit each aspect ratio, with focal point preserved + composition rebuilt
- **Element library**: brand mark, headline, visual subject — pulled and recomposed per format

### Variant generation checklist

- Focal point preserved (subject visible in every variant)
- Headline scales appropriately (short headline can survive square crops; long headlines may need word breaks)
- Brand mark visible in every variant
- Color and type consistency
- Each variant tested at its target glance time (1.5 sec for OOH; 3 sec for hero; etc.)

## Static design AI tells

Patterns that signal AI-generated static design and should be caught in QA.

| Tell | What to look for | Repair |
|---|---|---|
| **Generic "modern minimal" aesthetic** | Sterile, no specificity, looks like every other AI image | Re-prompt with named style reference; or composite with brand-specific elements |
| **Plastic skin / faces** | Smooth where real skin has texture; symmetrical features | Reroll or composite real photography |
| **Hand mistakes** | 6 fingers, fused fingers, wrong proportions | Reroll specifically the hand region; or hand-correct in Photoshop |
| **Gibberish text** | AI-generated signs / labels / books with nonsense text | Replace text in post-production |
| **Inconsistent lighting** | Subject lit one way, background another | Reroll or hand-relight |
| **Background-foreground perspective mismatch** | Subject in foreground doesn't match background perspective | Reroll with stronger spatial prompts |
| **Over-symmetric** | Mirrored faces / eyes; perfect symmetry where there shouldn't be | Slight asymmetry in post-production |
| **Stock-photo lighting** | Flat, even, lifeless illumination | Re-prompt with dramatic lighting reference (golden hour, harsh side light, etc.) |
| **Pattern repetition** | Repeating textures, identical clones in a crowd | Reroll or composite |
| **Sticker-edge artifacts** | Subject cut out with visible edge / fringe | Hand-mask edge or reroll |
